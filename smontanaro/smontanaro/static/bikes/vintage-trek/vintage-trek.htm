Introduction

Trek craftsmen Mike Apple and Dick Nolan. This photo appeared in Trek brochures from 1976 through 1981.

Welcome to the highly unofficial Vintage Trek* bicycle web site. The purpose of this site is to gather and disseminate information about steel-framed road bikes made by the Trek Bicycle Corporation, Waterloo, Wisconsin. This is a not-for-profit, hobbyist site.

In various bicycle-related Internet discussion forums, I have seen questions like:

  • How old is this Trek?
  • How was it equipped when new?
  • What was the original selling price?
  • Where was it in the price/quality hierarchy?

The query usually goes unanswered. This site is an attempt to provide the information that will answer questions such as these.

Trek TX900 headbadge. Photo courtesy Chas. Porter

What is on this site?

There are twelve early Trek Brochures that can be viewed on the site or downloaded as PDFs. There also are four early price lists from bike shops in Santa Barbara, CA.

There is a timeline for steel Trek road bikes that will get more accurate and complete as visitors kindly send me their comments, corrections, and materials. I hope to add a list of serial numbers by model and year.

The first seven Trek brochures roughly cover the period 1976 to 1982. They have no dates on them, but a collective best guess is 1976, 1978, 1979, 1980, 1981, 1981.5 and 1982. The last five brochures are copyrighted 1983, 1984, 1985 1986 and 1987. The price lists are dated: May 1, 1978; November 15, 1978; March 24, 1980, and April 1982.

My Trek

I have a Trek TX900, originally sold new in 1976 or 1977 to a very accomplished teenager who raced it in and around Santa Barbara, CA. He had equipped it mostly with Campagnolo Nuovo Record parts from 1973 and earlier, a Zeus front hub, and a Super Olympic rear hub. I purchased it from him in 1978 for the sum of $375. He sold the bike at age 16, after discovering girls and cars.

At the local bicycle shop in Santa Barbara, at about the time of my purchase, I picked up a Trek brochure which described the frame. I recently found that brochure in my old papers. I was surprised to find two other Trek brochures and four price lists that I had salted away as well. These materials were the genesis of this site.
