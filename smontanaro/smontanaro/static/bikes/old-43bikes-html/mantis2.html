1990 Mantis Valkyrie

Other than Yetis, Richard Cunningham's Mantis bikes were among the only long front center, neutral but fast, steeper head angle/shorter fork rake mountain bikes to be made in the '80s. Yeti and Mantis bikes were ountumbered thousandsfold by short, slack production bike designs during the 1980s... which have thankfully waggled their way toward extinction. So we have these southern California builders to thank for pointing us toward today's basicly ironed-out off-road geometry. (I say more about this elsewhere.)

And before mentioning all the dang triangles on this bike, it is the geometry and handling of the Mantis that is maybe their greatest innovation ... these are just flawless on fast tight singletrack.

Since 5 yrs ago when this was an incomplete bike sitting out in the rain in front of a pawn shop in Olympia, Wa. I've done a semi-accurate restoration of it. Strangely, ten years previous I was riding a lot in that same area (see Yeti pics) w/ 2 buddies who raced for Mantis and had XCR Composites and Valkyries. Capitol Forest had Mantises crawling all over it !! But this was purely a coincidence that this bike turned up there around 2000.
