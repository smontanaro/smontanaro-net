MAVIC is the UHOHU. The history of Mavic is fascinating, starting with the small outfit making mudguards in 1930 through their huge strides made in racing and innovation every decade since. Though a small, family business most of that time, their role in racing and innovation has been gigantic. Check out the history on their official site. Mavic now

I always dug the Mavic SSC group back in the '80s, especially on Sean Kelly's and "Lucho" Herrera's Tour bikes, but around here (Arizona) I only saw the rims and shifters; full Mavic groups were more expensive and far more scarce than Super Record was.

But it was most of this era's race bikes (including my old Walton with GEL280 sewups and MA40 clinchers) that sported the hard-anodized rims for which Mavic is now legendary. Check the 1956 PLX-10 ... we're in our 6th decade of racing on Mavic rims.

The '80s SSC group is scarce but when found, it trades at prices lower than it was 15-20 yrs ago. For now there's only a small fan base of people like me with a fetish for these parts, so they are usually cheaper than the Campagnolo equivalent. Some exceptions are below.
